# Power-Electronics-Modelling-of-the-BMW-i4-eDrive35-Gran-Coupe

System-level modelling and simulation of the BMW i4 eDrive35 electric drivetrain, including vehicle energy analysis, traction inverter design, and power electronics performance evaluation.

## Overview

This project was completed as a team effort and divided into three technical phases:

1. Vehicle Energy Modelling
2. Traction Inverter Design
3. On-Board Charger Design

My primary responsibility was the development and completion of Phases 1 and 2.

## Technical Summary

The project evaluated the BMW i4 eDrive35 powertrain through vehicle energy modelling, the design of a 210 kW SiC-based traction inverter and 7kW onboard charger. The inverter was analysed using SVPWM control, loss modelling, thermal evaluation, and simulation validation. A 7 kW onboard charger consisting of a PFC boost stage and isolated DC/DC converter was also developed as part of the overall vehicle charging architecture.
